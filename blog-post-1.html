BandBuddy: Building a Musician-Matching Platform

Posted on August 23, 2025 • 6 min read

BandBuddy is a simple idea that fixes a boring problem: finding the right people to play with is harder than actually making the music. I built a small web app where musicians post what they need, players show what they can offer, and the two collide in a feed that prioritises relevance instead of noise.

Why I Built It

Traditional forums are chaotic, group chats vanish into the ether, and “looking for guitarist” posts age badly. I wanted a space where a bassist who likes shoegaze doesn’t have to sift through function-band ads, and a singer can find three local projects in five minutes, not five weeks.

How It Works

There are two core objects in the feed: posts (general updates, demos, gig notices) and band ads (concrete roles with a deadline). Anyone can browse. If you want to like a post or register interest in a role, you create an account. The feed then splits into a general stream and a For You stream that leans into your preferences.

Personalised Discovery

Users set tag preferences on their profile. Tags cover genres, roles, influences and location hints. The For You feed boosts content that matches those tags so a drummer who loves math rock actually sees math rock. Posts can include an image and a single tag for now. The database supports multiple tags per post, so expanding that is straightforward later.

Band Ads With Intent

Creating a band ad forces clarity: band name, short description, target role, and an optional deadline for interest. People can register interest in one click. It’s faster than drafting the same paragraph across six platforms and hoping someone replies this month.

Design Choices

I wanted one unified stream instead of separate “marketplace” and “social” views. Both posts and ads appear together, but they’re visually distinct. Buttons and contrast meet accessibility guidelines, and the feed layout adapts cleanly on mobile so you can scroll at rehearsal without squinting.

Under the Hood

The stack is intentionally lightweight. Account creation includes basic validation to stop duplicate emails and weak passwords. Likes update in real time so engagement feels alive, not stale. The tagging model is built as many-to-many for flexibility, and the personalised feed logic sits cleanly beside the general feed to keep maintenance sane.

What’s Next

Two upgrades are queued: direct messaging so you don’t have to jump to another app after matching, and comments for posts to build an actual community. Multi-tag posts and better location filters are also on deck once the core loop has enough data.

Outcome

BandBuddy trims the search phase so people can get back to writing and playing. It’s not trying to be a social network for everything. It’s focused, opinionated and fast. Exactly what a band needs when the drummer is already late.
